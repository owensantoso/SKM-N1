Overview

This chapter focuses on expressions related to dependency and independence in Japanese. It explores various grammar points that describe how outcomes are influenced by certain conditions or remain unaffected by them. Understanding these structures is essential for conveying nuanced meanings in both spoken and written Japanese.

Each grammar point has specific contexts where it’s preferred, and there are subtle differences in how each point conveys dependency, generality, or independence. Here's a brief overview of the five main grammar points in this chapter:

  1. ～いかんだ: Indicates that the outcome depends on a certain condition.
  2. ～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず: Indicates that the outcome is not affected by certain conditions.
  3. ～をものともせず（に）: Indicates overcoming obstacles or challenges to take action.
  4. ～をよそに: Indicates acting without considering certain factors.
  5. ～ならいざしらず: Indicates conditional acceptability with contrasting outcomes.

1. ～いかんだ

Meaning: "Depending on...; It depends on..."

Japanese: 「～がどうであるかによって事態が変わる・事態が決まる。」

Usage: Indicates that the outcome or situation changes based on certain conditions. It is often used to express that success, continuation, or any other outcome depends on specific factors.

Formality: Neutral to formal. Suitable for both spoken and written Japanese.

Structure: 名詞 + いかんだ

Conjugation Examples:

  • 名詞 + いかんだ: 世界選手権大会をこの国で開催できるかどうかは、国民の協力いかんだ。
    "Whether we can host the World Championships in this country depends on the cooperation of the people."
  • 名詞 + いかんだ: 筆記試験はパスした。あしたの面接の結果いかんで採用が決まるそうだ。
    "I passed the written exam. It seems that whether I get hired depends on the results of tomorrow's interview."

Example Sentences:

  • 世界選手権大会をこの国で開催できるかどうかは、国民の協力いかんだ。
    "Whether we can host the World Championships in this country depends on the cooperation of the people."
  • 筆記試験はパスした。あしたの面接の結果いかんで採用が決まるそうだ。
    "I passed the written exam. It seems that whether I get hired depends on the results of tomorrow's interview."
  • 申し込み者数いかんでは、ツアーを中止しなければならないかもしれない。
    "Depending on the number of applicants, we might have to cancel the tour."
  • 支持率いかんでは、今の政権も長くは続かないだろう。
    "Depending on the approval rating, the current administration probably won't last long."

Comparison with Other Grammar Structures:

～いかんだ vs. ～にかかわらず

～にかかわらず is used similarly to ～いかんだ to indicate that something is not affected by certain conditions. However, ～いかんだ emphasizes dependency, whereas ～にかかわらず emphasizes independence.

  • ～いかんだ:
    "成功するかどうかは努力いかんだ。"
    "Whether we succeed or not depends on our efforts."
  • ～にかかわらず:
    "天候にかかわらず、試合は行われる。"
    "The match will proceed regardless of the weather."

Simpler Version

This grammar point is used to express dependency by highlighting a condition that affects the outcome. In everyday conversation, people might use simpler structures like ～次第だ.

  • Original (～いかんだ):
    世界選手権大会をこの国で開催できるかどうかは、国民の協力いかんだ。
    "Whether we can host the World Championships in this country depends on the cooperation of the people."
  • Simpler Version:
    世界選手権大会をこの国で開催できるかどうかは、国民の協力次第だ。
    "Whether we can host the World Championships in this country depends on the cooperation of the people."

Nuance Difference: The original ～いかんだ emphasizes the dependency on the condition, often with a formal tone. The simpler version using ～次第だ conveys the same meaning but in a more conversational and flexible manner.

Notes:

  • Used to express that an outcome is contingent upon a specific condition.
  • Often appears in formal or written contexts.
  • Commonly used with nouns that represent conditions, factors, or criteria.

2. ～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず

Meaning: "Regardless of...; Irrespective of..."

Japanese: 「～でも…でも関係なく、同じ種類のものにはみんな同じことが言える」

Usage: Indicates that the outcome or situation is not affected by certain conditions. It is used to express that something applies universally, regardless of the specified factors.

Formality: Neutral to formal. Suitable for both spoken and written Japanese, particularly in formal contexts.

Structure:

  • 名詞 + いかんにかかわらず
  • 名詞 + いかんによらず
  • 名詞 + いかんを問わず

Conjugation Examples:

  • 名詞 + いかんにかかわらず: 内容のいかんにかかわらず、個人情報のお問い合わせにはお答えしておりません。
    "Regardless of the content, we do not respond to inquiries about personal information."
  • 名詞 + いかんによらず: 明日の試合の結果いかんによらず、優勝できないことは決まってしまった。
    "Regardless of tomorrow's game results, it's decided that we cannot win the championship."
  • 名詞 + いかんを問わず: 当社は学歴・年齢・過去の実績のいかんを問わず、初任給は一律です。
    "Our company offers a uniform starting salary regardless of educational background, age, or past achievements."

Example Sentences:

  • 内容のいかんにかかわらず、個人情報のお問い合わせにはお答えしておりません。
    "Regardless of the content, we do not respond to inquiries about personal information."
  • 明日の試合の結果いかんによらず、優勝できないことは決まってしまった。
    "Regardless of tomorrow's game results, it's decided that we cannot win the championship."
  • 当社は学歴・年齢・過去の実績のいかんを問わず、初任給は一律です。
    "Our company offers a uniform starting salary regardless of educational background, age, or past achievements."

Comparison with Other Grammar Structures:

～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず vs. ～にかかわらず

～にかかわらず is a more general form to express "regardless of," while ～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず offer nuanced variations based on formality and context.

  • ～にかかわらず:
    "天候にかかわらず、イベントは開催されます。"
    "The event will proceed regardless of the weather."
  • ～いかんにかかわらず:
    "内容のいかんにかかわらず、全員に通知します。"
    "Regardless of the content, we will notify everyone."
  • ～いかんによらず:
    "結果いかんによらず、計画は実行されます。"
    "Regardless of the results, the plan will be executed."
  • ～いかんを問わず:
    "年齢いかんを問わず、応募可能です。"
    "Applicants of all ages are eligible."

Simpler Version

This grammar point is used to express that something is not affected by certain conditions by listing multiple forms. In everyday conversation, people might use simpler structures like ～も…も.

  • Original (～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず):
    内容のいかんにかかわらず、個人情報のお問い合わせにはお答えしておりません。
    "Regardless of the content, we do not respond to inquiries about personal information."
  • Simpler Version:
    内容にかかわらず、個人情報のお問い合わせにはお答えしておりません。
    "Regardless of the content, we do not respond to inquiries about personal information."

Nuance Difference: The original ～いかんにかかわらず・～いかんによらず・～いかんを問わず provides a formal and comprehensive way to express "regardless of," often used in written or formal contexts. The simpler version using ～にかかわらず is more conversational and flexible.

Notes:

  • Used to express that an outcome is unaffected by certain conditions.
  • Often appears in formal or written contexts.
  • Can be used with various nouns representing conditions, factors, or criteria.
  • Different variations (～いかんにかかわらず, ～いかんによらず, ～いかんを問わず) may be chosen based on nuance and formality.

3. ～をものともせず（に）

Meaning: "Undeterred by...; Despite..."

Japanese: 「普通なら～という障害に気持ちが負けてしまうのに、それを乗り越えて行動する。」

Usage: Indicates overcoming obstacles or challenges to take action. It conveys a sense of resilience and determination despite difficulties.

Formality: Neutral to formal. Suitable for both spoken and written Japanese.

Structure: 名詞 + をものともせず（に）

Conjugation Examples:

  • 名詞 + をものともせず（に）: 彼は体の障害をものともせずに、精力的に活動している。
    "He is energetically active, undeterred by his physical disabilities."
  • 名詞 + をものともせず（に）: 母は強かった。がんの宣告をものともせず、最期まで明るくふるまった。
    "My mother was strong. Undeterred by her cancer diagnosis, she remained cheerful until the end."

Example Sentences:

  • 彼は体の障害をものともせずに、精力的に活動している。
    "He is energetically active, undeterred by his physical disabilities."
  • 母は強かった。がんの宣告をものともせず、最期まで明るくふるまった。
    "My mother was strong. Undeterred by her cancer diagnosis, she remained cheerful until the end."
  • 隊員たちは危険をものともせずに、行方不明の人の捜索を続けた。
    "The team members continued searching for the missing person, undeterred by the danger."

Comparison with Other Grammar Structures:

～をものともせず（に） vs. ～にもかかわらず

～にもかかわらず means "in spite of" and is used to express that something happened despite certain conditions. ～をものともせず（に） emphasizes overcoming obstacles to take action.

  • ～をものともせず（に）:
    "彼は体の障害をものともせずに、精力的に活動している。"
    "He is energetically active, undeterred by his physical disabilities."
  • ～にもかかわらず:
    "天気が悪いにもかかわらず、試合は行われた。"
    "The match was held despite the bad weather."

Simpler Version

This grammar point is used to express overcoming obstacles by emphasizing resilience. In everyday conversation, people might use simpler structures like ～でも or ～にもかかわらず.

  • Original (～をものともせず（に）):
    彼は体の障害をものともせずに、精力的に活動している。
    "He is energetically active, undeterred by his physical disabilities."
  • Simpler Version:
    彼は体の障害があっても、精力的に活動している。
    "Even though he has physical disabilities, he is energetically active."

Nuance Difference: The original ～をものともせず（に） emphasizes the action of overcoming obstacles, often with a stronger sense of determination. The simpler version using ～があっても or ～にもかかわらず conveys the same idea but in a more straightforward and conversational manner.

Notes:

  • Used to express resilience and determination despite significant obstacles.
  • Often paired with nouns representing major challenges or difficulties (e.g., disease, danger, natural disasters).
  • Not typically used to describe minor obstacles.

4. ～をよそに

Meaning: "Ignoring...; Disregarding..."

Japanese: 「～は自分とは無関係であるかのように、少しも気にしないで行動する。」

Usage: Indicates acting without considering certain factors, as if they are unrelated. It often conveys a sense of surprise, disbelief, or indifference.

Formality: Neutral to formal. Suitable for both spoken and written Japanese.

Structure: 名詞 + をよそに

Conjugation Examples:

  • 名詞 + をよそに: 家族の心配をよそに、子供は退院したその日から友達と遊びに出かけた。
    "Ignoring his family's worries, the child went out to play with friends the day he was discharged."
  • 名詞 + をよそに: 住民たちの抗議行動をよそに、ダムの建設計画が進められている。
    "Ignoring the residents' protests, the dam construction plan is progressing."

Example Sentences:

  • 家族の心配をよそに、子供は退院したその日から友達と遊びに出かけた。
    "Ignoring his family's worries, the child went out to play with friends the day he was discharged."
  • 住民たちの抗議行動をよそに、ダムの建設計画が進められている。
    "Ignoring the residents' protests, the dam construction plan is progressing."
  • 彼は周囲の人たちの不安をよそに、再び戦地の取材に出発していった。
    "Ignoring the anxieties of those around him, he set off again to cover the war zone."

Comparison with Other Grammar Structures:

～をよそに vs. ～にもかかわらず

～にもかかわらず means "in spite of" and is used to express that something happened despite certain conditions. ～をよそに emphasizes acting as if the condition is unrelated, often with a sense of surprise or disbelief.

  • ～をよそに:
    "住民たちの抗議行動をよそに、ダムの建設計画が進められている。"
    "Ignoring the residents' protests, the dam construction plan is progressing."
  • ～にもかかわらず:
    "天気が悪いにもかかわらず、試合は行われた。"
    "The match was held despite the bad weather."

Simpler Version

This grammar point is used to express that someone is acting without considering certain factors, often in a way that surprises or disappoints others. In everyday conversation, people might use simpler structures like ～を無視して or ～を気にせず.

  • Original (～をよそに):
    家族の心配をよそに、子供は退院したその日から友達と遊びに出かけた。
    "Ignoring his family's worries, the child went out to play with friends the day he was discharged."
  • Simpler Version:
    家族の心配を無視して、子供は退院したその日から友達と遊びに出かけた。
    "Ignoring his family's worries, the child went out to play with friends the day he was discharged."

Nuance Difference: The original ～をよそに conveys a stronger sense of acting as if the condition is completely unrelated, often with an emotional undertone. The simpler version using ～を無視して is more direct and commonly used in conversational Japanese.

Notes:

  • Used to describe actions taken without regard to certain factors, often implying surprise or disapproval.
  • Typically used with nouns representing concerns, protests, or expectations.
  • Does not apply when the subject is the speaker themselves.

5. ～ならいざしらず

Meaning: "If it's... then fine, but..."

Japanese: 「～ならそうかもしれないが、全く違う状況なのだから結果も違う。」

Usage: Indicates that while something might be acceptable under certain conditions, it is not under others. It often introduces a contrasting situation or outcome.

Formality: Neutral to formal. Suitable for both spoken and written Japanese.

Structure:

  • 動詞普通形 + ならいざしらず
  • 名詞（の） + ならいざしらず
  • 形容詞（形だーなの・宿だーなの） + ならいざしらず

Conjugation Examples:

  • 動詞普通形 + ならいざしらず: ヒマラヤ登山をするのならいざしらず、その辺の山へ行くのにそんなに重装備でなければいけないのか。
    "If it's about climbing the Himalayas, then fine, but why do I have to carry such heavy equipment for hiking those nearby mountains?"
  • 名詞（の） + ならいざしらず: 祖父母の代ならいざしらず、今の時代に「手ぬぐい」なんてあまり使わないよ。
    "If it's the grandparents' generation, then fine, but people in today's era don't really use things like tenugui much."
  • 形容詞（宿だーなの） + ならいざしらず: 宿が快適ならいざしらず、部屋の清潔さには欠けている。
    "If the lodging is comfortable, then fine, but it lacks cleanliness."

Example Sentences:

  • 安いホテルならいざしらず、一流ホテルでこんなにサービスが悪いなんて許せない。
    "If it's an inexpensive hotel, then fine, but I can't forgive such poor service at a top-tier hotel."
  • 祖父母の代ならいざしらず、今の時代に「手ぬぐい」なんてあまり使わないよ。
    "If it's the grandparents' generation, then fine, but people in today's era don't really use things like tenugui much."
  • ヒマラヤ登山をするのならいざしらず、その辺の山へ行くのにそんなに重装備でなければいけないのか。
    "If it's about climbing the Himalayas, then fine, but why do I have to carry such heavy equipment for hiking those nearby mountains?"
  • 治療が難しいのならいざしらず、よくある病気ですから、そんなに心配することはありませんよ。
    "If the treatment is difficult, then fine, but since it's a common illness, there's no need to worry so much."

Comparison with Other Grammar Structures:

～ならいざしらず vs. ～なら

～なら is a conditional form meaning "if," used to state a condition and its result. ～ならいざしらず adds an element of contrast, implying that the condition is acceptable, but the situation is different.

  • ～なら:
    "雨が降るなら、出かけません。"
    "If it rains, I won't go out."
  • ～ならいざしらず:
    "安いホテルならいざしらず、一流ホテルでこんなにサービスが悪いなんて許せない。"
    "If it's an inexpensive hotel, then fine, but I can't forgive such poor service at a top-tier hotel."

Simpler Version

This grammar point is used to express conditional acceptability with a contrasting outcome. In everyday conversation, people might use simpler structures like ～なら…でも.

  • Original (～ならいざしらず):
    安いホテルならいざしらず、一流ホテルでこんなにサービスが悪いなんて許せない。
    "If it's an inexpensive hotel, then fine, but I can't forgive such poor service at a top-tier hotel."
  • Simpler Version:
    安いホテルならいいけど、一流ホテルでこんなにサービスが悪いなんて許せない。
    "It's fine if it's an inexpensive hotel, but I can't forgive such poor service at a top-tier hotel."

Nuance Difference: The original ～ならいざしらず carries a slightly more formal and emphatic tone, whereas the simpler version using ～ならいいけど is more conversational and straightforward.

Notes:

  • Used to express that while something may be acceptable under certain conditions, it is not acceptable in other, often contrasting, conditions.
  • Commonly used with nouns or verbs that present clear contrasts.
  • Often paired with negative outcomes in the contrasting condition.
  • Typically used in formal or written contexts to convey strong opinions or criticisms.
